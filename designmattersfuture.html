the typewriter

It is hard to say with certainty where typewriters will be in the far future. Following the trends of today, they are most likely to remain collectibles and rise in price as they become older and more rare. This could also lead to them potentially being featured in more museums and gaining prominence as antiques.

Typing on an actual typewriter on paper is only a softer version of chiseling words into stone. --tom hanks

4 future

Since modern technology puts emphasis on devices featuring many abilities while still sporting a slim and lightweight exterior, it is unlikely that typewriters will become widely manufactured again. With the absence of a market for them, it would not be logical to mass produce something that is pricey and inconvenient. This is not to say that typewriter enthusiasts should feel discouraged; there is still a passionate -- albeit small -- community maintaining their own collections and supporting modest companies that still produce typewriters for sale (Millard). If these typewriter fanatics exist in a world where slim, powerful devices are favored, it might be safe to assume that they will still be present in years to come. Though technology may always be advancing, this will only cause people to cherish typewriters even more.
