the typewriter

Invented over a century ago, the typewriter has a long history and has undoubtedly paved the way for much of the technology we use today. Responsible for the first keyboard and the QWERTY order that has maintained its status as the standard layout since the 1870s, it acts as an iconic model in the design world. Though it might not be as popular as it once was, it has certainly left its impact as a device that allows individuals to quickly record their thoughts, send correspondence, and so on. Typewriters were also favored by many famous authors, including Ernest Hemingway, Agatha Christie, and E.B. White. To this day, even well known actor Tom Hanks has a collection exceeding fifty typewriters.

1 overview

There are many debates about who invented the first typewriter before it was commercially successful, due to various claims without drawings or other proof. The first effective prototype -- which currently sits at the Smithsonian Institution -- was invented by Christopher Latham Sholes in 1867. It would not create a stir in the market until much later after its release; however, soon enough, typewriters were in every workplace and household. They rode the wave of success throughout the early 1900s until the late sixties, when the efficiency and technological advancements of computers took their place. Today, typewriters mostly serve as a spark of nostalgia -- a reminder of both the aesthetics and inventions of the past. Nonetheless, it is an unforgettable device that has more than served its purpose and demonstrated how one design can hold influence beyond its years.
