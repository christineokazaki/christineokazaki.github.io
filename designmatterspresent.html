the typewriter

Today, typewriters have been largely replaced by computers, laptops, and tablets. The convenience and efficiency of these devices are ultimately much greater than a machine whose last upgrades were made in the 1900s. Nonetheless, this does not mean that typewriters have completely disappeared; rather, they have become collectors items, with a particular resurgence in recent years.

3 present

Some areas are more reliant on typewriters than others. In India, for example, some people still sit on the street and type out documents for others in exchange for a small fee, and this exists as their occupation. Though their last typewriter manufacturing site was closed down in 2011, this has not stopped these men from sitting outside of government buildings and typing up wills, deeds, and applications in order to make a living. Many of their clients come to them with illegible notes, which the typists are easily able to decipher -- this being their advantage to what they refer to as “computer people” -- and turn into a cleanly typed sheet of paper (Tarafdar). Furthermore, the NYPD only started to phase out typewriters at the end of 2015 (Millard)

As they are no longer mass produced, typewriters are rare in American households these days; despite this, they are fairly accessible and available for purchase through the Internet. Though they are not as practical as an easily portable laptop or tablet, there are several advantages to typewriters. Without internet access or external abilities, it minimizes distractions and presents a challenge to the user, not only to stay focused but also to think carefully about their words, since you cannot erase. Furthermore, they offer more privacy and many do not require electricity -- which is why they are more popular in countries like India and Latin America (Millard). Some also prefer typewriters for their sentimental ttachments, and the sensory feedback and deeper connection that they provide; beyond this, others might simply appreciate the retro, vintage aesthetic that seems to be making a comeback recently.
